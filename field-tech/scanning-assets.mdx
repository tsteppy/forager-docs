---
title: "Scanning Assets"
description: "How to confirm asset locations during your normal IT work"
---

The Forager app records asset locations as a side effect of your existing work. You do not need to run a dedicated audit — just scan barcodes when you're already near equipment.

## Hardware barcode scanners

If you're on a Zebra or Honeywell device, the hardware trigger works automatically — no setup needed. The app detects the scanner at startup and switches to hardware mode.

In hardware mode, you will see **"Waiting for scan…"** instead of a camera preview. Pull the trigger to scan as usual. The match/mismatch result appears exactly the same as it would after a camera scan.

If your admin has enabled the camera fallback option, you will also see a **"Use camera instead"** button. Tap it if you need to scan a barcode the hardware scanner is struggling with.

If your device has a hardware scanner but the app is showing a camera preview, check with your admin — the input mode may be set to Camera.

The first time you open the app in hardware scanner mode, a one-time notification appears at the bottom of the home screen confirming that hardware mode is active. It dismisses automatically after a few seconds.

## Photo attestation

If your company has photo attestation enabled, Forager automatically captures a photo of the asset tag at the moment the barcode is detected. You do not need to do anything — the photo is taken silently in the background and attached to the attestation record.

Your admin can review these photos in the Attestation Log on the web dashboard. The photos serve as physical proof of presence for compliance audits.

<Note>
  Photo capture only works in camera mode. If you are on a hardware scanner device, no photo is taken — your attestation is recorded normally without one.
</Note>

## Before you start

- Your location must be surveyed. If the app cannot determine your current room, it will tell you.
- You need a line-of-sight to the asset's barcode label.

## How to scan

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the app and wait for location lock">
    Open Forager. The app begins scanning Wi-Fi and Bluetooth signals in the background. Wait for the **current room** indicator to show a room name. This usually takes 5–15 seconds.

    If the indicator shows "Locating…" for more than 30 seconds, see [App not finding my location](/troubleshooting/app-location).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scan the asset tag">
    Tap the scan button. By default, the app is in **Barcode Mode** and accepts most standard formats (QR, Code 128, Code 39).

    **No barcodes? No problem!** If your facility uses plain text tags (like Brother P-Touch labels), simply tap the **Text Label** toggle at the top of the camera screen. The app will automatically read the text using Optical Character Recognition (OCR) and log it!
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the result">
    The app shows one of three outcomes:
    - **Match** — the asset is where it's supposed to be. A confirmation is recorded automatically.
    - **Mismatch** — the asset was last recorded in a different room. See below.
    - **New asset** — the asset tag is not in Forager yet. The app records its current location as the first known location.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Handling a mismatch

A mismatch means the asset's recorded location does not match the room you're currently in.

<Note>
  Mismatches are normal. Equipment gets moved. The important thing is to resolve them accurately.
</Note>

When a mismatch appears, you have two options:

**Update location** — confirm the asset is actually here now. Forager updates the recorded location and writes a `mismatch_confirmed` attestation. Use this when the device has genuinely been moved.

**Dismiss** — leave the recorded location unchanged. Forager writes a `mismatch_dismissed` attestation. Use this when you picked up the device to service it and will return it to its original location, or when you believe the current room classification is incorrect.

## Overriding your detected room

If the app shows the wrong room in the location chip, you can manually select your actual room before scanning:

1. Tap the **room chip** at the top of the Presence Confirmation screen
2. A room picker sheet opens — select the correct room
3. The chip updates and all subsequent scans use your selected room

Use this when RF detection is uncertain (low confidence, wrong room shown) or when surveying hasn't been completed for your area. Your manual selection is not saved after you leave the screen.

## Tips for accurate scanning

- Stand still when the app is determining your location — walking between rooms while scanning can cause the app to classify you in the wrong room
- Scan assets in the room they live in, not from a hallway or doorway
- If you moved a device for repair, scan it again when you return it

## Points earned sheet

After each successful confirmation, a sheet pops up showing what you earned:

| Badge | Points |
|---|---|
| 🔥 | 10 pts — asset never confirmed or not seen in 90+ days |
| ⚡ | 3 pts — not confirmed in 30–89 days |
| ✓ | 1 pt — confirmed within the last 30 days |

Tap **Done** to return to scanning mode. Tap **Next in queue →** to jump to the Most Wanted list and pick the next target without losing your momentum.

## Offline scanning

Forager can record scans without an internet connection. Attestations are queued locally and synced when connectivity is restored. The room classification still works offline since it uses local RF fingerprints.
