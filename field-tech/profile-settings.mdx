## Display Name

Your display name appears on every attestation and calibration you record. It is the name other users see in audit logs and reports.

To change it:

1. Clear the **Display Name** field and type a new name
2. Tap **Save Display Name**

The change takes effect immediately for all new records. Existing records are not retroactively updated.

## Account

Read-only information about your account:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Company** | The organization your account belongs to |
| **Username** | Your unique login identifier |
| **Email** | The email address associated with your account |
| **Role** | Your permission level — Field Tech, Team Lead, or Administrator |

Contact your admin to change your role or move your account to a different company.

## Appearance

Controls the visual theme of the app.

- **System default** — Follows your device's light/dark mode setting
- **Light** — Always use the light theme
- **Dark** — Always use the dark theme

The change applies immediately throughout the app.

## Preferences

### Haptic Feedback

When enabled, the phone vibrates briefly on scan events — a confirmation tap when an asset matches, a rejection pulse when it doesn't. Disable this if vibration is disruptive in your environment.

**Default:** On

### Audio Cues

When enabled, the app plays short sounds on scan events alongside (or instead of) haptic feedback. Useful in environments where you can't feel vibration but can hear audio through an earpiece.

**Default:** Off

## Help & Session

| Action | Description |
|---|---|
| **Support** | Opens a support request form to contact the Harbinge.rs team |
| **Sign out** | Signs you out of Forager and returns to the login screen |

Signing out does not delete any locally cached data. When you sign back in, your settings and attestation history are restored.

## Security

### Biometric App Lock

When enabled, Forager requires fingerprint, face, or PIN authentication each time you open the app from a cold start. This protects your session if your device is lost or accessed by someone else.
