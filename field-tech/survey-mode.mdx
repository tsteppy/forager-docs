---
title: "Survey Mode"
description: "How to survey rooms so the app can determine where you are"
---

Survey Mode is how you teach Forager what each room looks like on the Wi-Fi and Bluetooth spectrum. Without a survey, the app cannot determine your current location — and without your location, presence confirmation does not work.

You only need to survey each room once. Re-survey if the room's layout or network equipment changes significantly, or if the app consistently misidentifies the room.

## What survey captures

When you run a survey capture, the app records 4–6 samples of every Wi-Fi access point and Bluetooth device visible from that spot. It also records barometric pressure (for floor estimation) and magnetic field strength (a stable per-room characteristic based on local metal and structural features). These signals together form a fingerprint unique to that room.

## Running a survey

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Survey Mode">
    From the Forager home screen, tap **Survey Mode**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the floor">
    Use the floor selector at the top to choose which floor you are on. Rooms are grouped by urgency — **Needs Attention** rooms appear first, followed by **Monitor**, then **Covered**. Each row shows the current sample count and days since the last survey.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the room">
    Physically walk into the room you want to survey. Stand near the center, away from doorways. Tap the room in the list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Capture">
    Tap the **Capture** button. The app records a set of RF samples — the sample count increments as each one completes. The full capture takes approximately 30 seconds. Stay still — walking or moving between rooms during capture degrades the fingerprint.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Repeat for each room on the floor">
    After the capture completes, tap **Back** to return to the room list and move to the next room. Work your way through every room on the floor.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reading the sample count

After a capture completes, the room row shows an updated sample count and quality category. More samples mean a tighter fingerprint and higher confidence during room classification. There is no fixed minimum, but rooms with 3+ captures from different positions in the room consistently reach **Covered** status.

If a capture completes but the sample count does not increase, the app may be having trouble scanning signals. Check that Wi-Fi and Bluetooth are enabled on your device.

## Adding a room during survey

If a room does not appear in the list, tap **Add New Room**, enter a name and level type (e.g. "Room", "Zone"), and it will be created under the selected floor. You can then survey it immediately.

## Pinning rooms to the floor plan

If a floor plan image has been uploaded by your admin, a floor plan view appears at the top of the Survey Mode screen. After completing a capture for a room, you can tap the floor plan to place a pin for that room's location. This pin is used for visual navigation during Device Hunt and mismatch display.

If no floor plan is uploaded, survey still works — rooms are tracked by name and location hierarchy only.

## Survey quality indicators

After surveying, open **Coverage** to see the quality of each room's fingerprint.

| Indicator | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Green | Well surveyed — ready for attestation |
| Yellow | Thin — fewer than ideal samples; consider re-surveying |
| Gray | Not yet surveyed |

<Note>
  The first survey of a new room creates its fingerprint. Subsequent surveys on the same room refresh it. If room conditions have not changed, you do not need to re-survey frequently — once per quarter is typical unless equipment or layout changes.
</Note>

## Tips for accurate surveys

- Survey each room from the middle of the room, not from a doorway or hallway
- Survey at a typical working time — signal density can vary if nearby equipment is off overnight
- If two adjacent rooms are frequently confused by the app, re-survey both from the center of each room with the door closed
- Rooms that share a wall with high-density networking equipment benefit from extra captures
