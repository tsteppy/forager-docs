## Overview

Each company account has a `subscription_status` that controls what its users can do in Forager. You update this status manually from the company detail page in the platform admin dashboard. There is no automated billing system — invoices are sent manually and status is updated by hand when payment is received or overdue.

## Subscription statuses

| Status | Customer experience | Database access |
|---|---|---|
| **Active** | Full access — normal operation | Read and write |
| **Grace Period** | Amber warning banner on every dashboard page. App and dashboard fully functional. | Read only — new scans, asset updates, and team changes are blocked |
| **Suspended** | Dashboard redirects to a suspension screen. App scans fail silently at the database layer. | No access |

The warning banner reads: *"Your subscription payment is overdue. Access will be restricted soon. Contact support."* It links to support@hiveriselabs.com.

The suspension screen reads: *"Your Forager account has been suspended due to a lapsed subscription. Your data is safe and will be restored when your account is reinstated."*

## Changing a company's status

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the company detail page">
    Go to `/admin` and click the company name. The detail page shows an activity dashboard and a **Billing** card at the top.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the new status">
    In the Billing card, select the desired status using the radio buttons:
    - **Active** — normal operation
    - **Grace Period (read-only)** — payment overdue, reads allowed, writes blocked
    - **Suspended (no access)** — full lockout

    The Save button is disabled until you change the selection from the current value.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The change takes effect immediately — no restart or cache clear needed. The next time any user from that company loads a page, they will see the updated behavior.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Recommended dunning workflow

When a renewal invoice goes unpaid, follow this sequence:

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| 0 | Send invoice (net-30 terms) |
| 7 | Send payment reminder email |
| 14 | Send warning email — account will enter read-only mode in 7 days |
| 21 | Set status → **Grace Period** in the admin dashboard |
| 30 | Set status → **Suspended** if still unpaid |

When payment is received, set status back to **Active** immediately. The customer regains full access on their next page load.

## What is and is not blocked in Grace Period

Grace Period is read-only at the database layer — the restriction is enforced by RLS policies, not just the UI. This means it applies to both the web dashboard and the Android app.

**Blocked in Grace Period:**
- Submitting new scans (Android app)
- Adding or updating asset records
- Confirming asset presence (attestations)
- Assigning team members to sites
- Modifying the location hierarchy

**Still accessible in Grace Period:**
- Viewing the dashboard, reports, floor plans, and asset registry
- Viewing team membership and location hierarchy
- Downloading existing data

This gives customers a strong incentive to pay while preserving their ability to review their data and contact you.

## Data retention

Customer data is never deleted when a company is suspended. All records remain intact and are immediately accessible when the account is reinstated. You can safely leave a company in Suspended status indefinitely without data loss risk.

## Company status in the company list

The `/admin` company list shows a color-coded status badge next to each company name:

- **Active** — green
- **Grace** — amber
- **Suspended** — red

This lets you see at a glance which accounts need attention.
