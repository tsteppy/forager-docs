## Overview

A Forager pilot runs from first contact through a customer's first full attestation cycle — typically 2–4 weeks. Your job as the onboarding admin is to provision the account, coach the customer's admin through setup, and verify each phase completes before handing off.

This guide is your runbook. Follow it in order. Each phase has a verification step — do not move on until it passes.

---

## Phase 0 — Pre-engagement checklist

Collect these from the customer before you touch the admin dashboard.

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Customer organization name** | Exactly as it should appear in the system |
| **Admin's name and email** | The person who will manage the Forager account |
| **Pilot scope** | Which building(s), floor(s), and approximate room count |
| **Asset source** | Do they have a CMDB (ServiceNow)? CSV export? Manual entry? |
| **Tech device count** | How many Android devices will field techs use |
| **Seat limit needed** | Admin + number of field techs (default 6; adjust for their team size) |
| **Floor plan files** | JPEG or PNG preferred. PDFs work but lose interactive pin support. |
| **Timeline** | Pilot start date and expected completion date |

<Note>
  For pilots, seat limit = 1 admin + number of field techs participating. You can raise it later directly in the database if needed. Default of 6 covers most small pilots.
</Note>

## Phase 1 — Provision the company

**Your actions — customer is not involved yet.**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log into the platform admin dashboard">
    Go to [dashboard.hiveriselabs.com/admin](https://dashboard.hiveriselabs.com/admin). You must be signed in with your Hiverise Labs admin account (`SUPER_ADMIN_EMAIL`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Add Company form">
    Click **Add Company**. The form expands inline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the form">
    - **Company name**: the customer's organization name
    - **Admin email**: the IT admin's email you collected in Phase 0
    - **Seat limit**: admin + field techs (e.g., 1 admin + 4 techs = 5 seats)
    - **Notes**: paste in your pre-engagement notes — contract type, pilot scope, contact name, start date. This is internal only.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit and confirm">
    Click **Create Company & Send Invite**. You should see a green banner: *"[Company name] created. Invite sent to [email]."*

    If you see an error instead:
    - **Email already has an account**: the admin already exists in Supabase auth — contact support to resolve
    - **Company name conflict**: choose a more specific name (e.g., include city or department)
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify before continuing:**

The invite email is sent immediately. Confirm you see the success banner. If the banner is absent or shows an error, do not proceed — the company row may or may not have been created.

## Phase 2 — Customer admin accepts the invite

**Their action, your coaching.**

Send the customer admin the following message (adapt as needed):

> *"I've set up your Forager account. Look for an email with the subject "You have been invited" — it may take a few minutes to arrive. Click the link inside and you'll be taken directly to a welcome page confirming your account is active. No password setup is required at this step.*
>
> *After that, go to [dashboard.hiveriselabs.com](https://dashboard.hiveriselabs.com) and sign in with this email address. You'll have full admin access."*

**What to watch for:**

- If they don't receive the email within 5 minutes, have them check spam. The sender is Supabase (no-reply@mail.app.supabase.io).
- If the invite link shows an error, the link may have expired (valid for 24 hours). You can re-invite by provisioning a new invite — this does not create a duplicate company, but currently requires a manual Supabase re-invite. Contact support.
- Once they accept, their profile is automatically seeded with `role: admin` for their company.

**Verify before continuing:**

Ask the admin to confirm they can log into [dashboard.hiveriselabs.com](https://dashboard.hiveriselabs.com) and see the **Team** page. If they land on an error or see no company data, the profile was not seeded — escalate.

## Phase 3 — Team setup

**Customer admin's actions. Coach them through this call or async.**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Admin invites field techs">
    In the dashboard → **Team** → **Invite Team Member** card. Enter each tech's email and send. Each tech receives an invite email.

    Techs do not need to set a password. When they click the link, they land on a welcome page with a **Get it on Google Play** button. They download the app and sign in with their invite email.

    Remind the admin: invites expire after 24 hours. If a tech doesn't accept in time, the admin can re-send from the Team page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm techs appear in the team list">
    After a tech accepts and signs in, they appear in the Team member list with role **User**. The admin does not need to do anything further — techs are ready to use the app.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify before continuing:**

At least one field tech account is confirmed — they appear in the Team list and can sign in to the Forager app.

## Phase 4 — Location hierarchy

**Customer admin's actions in the Forager app.**

Walk the admin through this if they are unfamiliar with the app. The hierarchy must be built top-down before survey can begin.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Build the location tree">
    In the Forager app → **Manage Locations**. Add nodes in this order:
    1. Campus or site (optional — skip for single-building pilots)
    2. Building
    3. Floor (one node per floor being piloted)
    4. Room (one node per room that will be tracked)

    For a typical pilot, you are scoping to **1–2 floors**. Keep it small — a full floor of 15 rooms is a good pilot scope.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload floor plans">
    For each floor node, upload a JPEG or PNG of the floor plan. This enables the visual map view in the dashboard and is strongly recommended for pilots — it makes the demo much more compelling.

    After uploading, the admin places each room's pin on the floor plan image. This takes about 5–10 minutes per floor and only needs to be done once.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify before continuing:**

Every room in the pilot scope has a node in the location tree. Floor plan images are uploaded and room pins are placed.

## Phase 5 — Survey

**Field techs run this. Customer admin coordinates.**

Survey is the most time-sensitive phase and the most common place pilots stall. Set expectations clearly: a tech needs to physically stand in each room for about 30 seconds. A 10–15 room floor takes 20–30 minutes total.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Brief the tech team">
    Share these instructions with the field techs before they start:
    - One person with the app is enough per room — they do not need to stay long
    - The app must have location permission and Bluetooth enabled
    - They move room to room, one capture per room
    - They do not need to scan any assets during survey — survey and attestation are separate steps
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tech runs survey for each room">
    In the app → **Survey Mode**:
    1. Select the floor
    2. Select the room
    3. Tap **Capture** and hold still for ~30 seconds
    4. Wait for the capture to complete, then move to the next room
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check coverage quality">
    In the app → **Coverage**. Each room shows:
    - **Green** — well surveyed, ready for attestation
    - **Yellow** — thin data, consider re-surveying
    - **Gray** — not yet surveyed

    All pilot rooms should be **green** before the first attestation run.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify before continuing:**

All rooms in the pilot scope show green in Coverage. If any show yellow after a re-survey, they can proceed — yellow rooms still work but may have slightly lower confidence.

## Phase 6 — Asset seeding

**Customer admin's actions in the dashboard.**

Assets need to exist in Forager before techs can scan them. There are two paths:

**Path A — CMDB import (preferred for pilots with an existing ServiceNow or CMDB)**

In the dashboard → **Assets** → **Import CSV**. The customer exports a CSV from their CMDB with columns: `asset_tag`, `name`, `description`, `serial_number`, `cmdb_id`, `cmdb_location`. Only `asset_tag` is required.

For the pilot, import only the assets in the rooms being surveyed — not their entire inventory. A focused set of 50–200 assets makes the pilot cleaner to validate.

**Path B — Manual or small test set**

If no CMDB is available, the customer can add assets manually in the dashboard, or you can prepare a small CSV with known asset tags for them to scan during validation.

**Verify before continuing:**

Assets appear in the dashboard Assets list. The customer can search for a specific asset tag and find it.

---

## Phase 7 — First attestation run

**Field tech's action. This is the pilot validation moment.**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tech enters a surveyed room">
    The tech opens the Forager app and walks into one of the surveyed rooms. The app should automatically detect the room from the RF fingerprint.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tech scans an asset barcode">
    The tech scans the barcode of a known asset in that room. The app:
    - Confirms the current room
    - Looks up the asset's recorded location
    - Records a confirmation (match) or flags a mismatch
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify in the dashboard">
    Log into [dashboard.hiveriselabs.com](https://dashboard.hiveriselabs.com) → **Floor Plan** → select the floor. The scanned room's pin should show the asset with a **green indicator** (confirmed within 7 days).

    You can also go to **Reports** and filter by today's date to see the attestation record with tech name, asset, room, and timestamp.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Pilot is live when:** at least one attestation appears in the dashboard from a real scan in a real room.

## Pilot health checks

After the first week of active use, review these with the customer admin:

| Check | What to look for |
|---|---|
| **Coverage drift** | Any rooms turning yellow or gray since survey? Re-survey if the physical layout changed. |
| **Mismatch rate** | A small number of mismatches is normal. A high rate suggests a room's survey needs a refresh or assets have physically moved. |
| **Inactive techs** | Team page → Last Active column. Techs with no recent activity may not have completed setup. |
| **Seat limit** | Team page shows member count vs. limit. If they need more seats, update the seat_limit directly in the database. |

## Common issues

| Symptom | Likely cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Admin never received invite email | Spam filter, or email typo during provisioning | Check spam; if typo, provision a new invite with correct email |
| Admin accepted invite but has no company data in dashboard | Profile seeding failed | Check that they clicked the email link (not the Supabase dashboard) and try signing out and back in |
| Tech accepted invite but app shows no company | Same profile seeding issue | Tech should sign out and sign back in |
| Room not detected during attestation | Room was not surveyed, or survey was too brief | Re-survey the room with a longer capture |
| Asset not found on scan | Asset tag not in the system | Check Assets list; import the missing asset |
| Seat limit error when inviting a tech | Company is at capacity | Increase seat_limit in the database |

---

## Pilot success criteria

A pilot is considered successful when:

- [ ] All rooms in scope are green in Coverage
- [ ] All pilot assets are loaded in the system
- [ ] At least one full team (admin + field techs) has active accounts
- [ ] Attestations appear in the dashboard from real scans
- [ ] The customer admin can pull a report showing confirmed asset locations

When all five are checked, the pilot is ready to convert to a production account. Seat limit can be raised and additional buildings onboarded following the same Phase 4–6 pattern.
